Cupid Ltd (CUPID:IN)

Cupid Ltd stands out as a niche global player in sexual wellness with expanding export reach and strong margin discipline.

Investment Thesis

  • Cupid Ltd is one of the few Indian manufacturers with WHO/UNFPA prequalification for both male and female condoms, providing a defensible global niche and strong entry barriers.
  • The company’s export-driven business model offers currency diversification and access to higher-margin institutional contracts with agencies like UNFPA and USAID.
  • Healthy balance sheet with no significant debt and consistent dividend payouts make Cupid a compelling small-cap defensive play in the sexual wellness segment.

Key Highlights

  • FY24 revenue growth expected at ~15% YoY, driven by higher export orders and product mix improvement toward female condoms.
  • EBITDA margins remain robust at ~28%, supported by efficient manufacturing and favorable raw material procurement.
  • Domestic retail expansion and diversification into lubricants and allied products provide incremental growth levers.

Valuation & Methodology

Cupid Ltd currently trades at an estimated FY25E P/E of ~14x, a discount to global peers in the sexual wellness space (typically 18–20x). Given its strong export franchise, debt-free balance sheet, and improving product mix, we believe this discount is unwarranted.

Our valuation is based on a blended approach using DCF and relative P/E multiples. We assign a target multiple of 18x FY26E EPS, implying a 12-month target price of ₹490. The valuation reflects sustainable earnings growth and margin visibility amid rising institutional demand.

Catalysts (Next 12 Months)

  • New supply contracts from UNFPA and African government procurement agencies.
  • Commercial rollout of new female condom variants and lubricants in domestic markets.
  • Potential capacity expansion and automation at Nashik facility improving throughput and margins.

Risks

  • Dependence on institutional contracts exposes revenue to tender timing and pricing volatility.
  • Raw material price fluctuations (latex) could compress margins if not offset by pricing power.
  • Limited brand visibility in domestic retail segment may constrain long-term growth diversification.
