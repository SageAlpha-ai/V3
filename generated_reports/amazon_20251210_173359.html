Update Note

Amazon (AMZN)

Amazon continues to leverage its scale and AI-driven infrastructure to drive margin expansion across retail, AWS, and advertising.

Investment Thesis

  • Amazon’s diversified business model—spanning e-commerce, cloud computing, and advertising—positions it as a structural winner in digital consumption and enterprise digitization.
  • Margin expansion is accelerating, driven by AWS growth, advertising monetization, and operational efficiencies in fulfillment and logistics.
  • Strong free cash flow generation and disciplined capital allocation underpin long-term shareholder value creation.

Key Highlights

  • AWS revenue growth reaccelerating to mid-teens as enterprise cloud demand stabilizes and generative AI workloads ramp up.
  • Advertising segment surpassing $50B annualized run rate, with higher-margin contribution improving consolidated profitability.
  • North America retail profitability improving through automation, regionalized fulfillment, and Prime membership pricing optimization.

Valuation & Methodology

We value Amazon using a sum-of-the-parts (SOTP) approach, applying distinct multiples to AWS, advertising, and retail operations. AWS is valued at 20x 2025E EBITDA, advertising at 18x, and retail at 10x. This yields an implied equity value of approximately $2.0 trillion, or $225 per share.

Relative valuation versus peers (Microsoft, Alphabet) suggests Amazon trades at a discount on forward EV/EBITDA despite superior free cash flow growth. We see upside as the market re-rates AWS and advertising as core profit drivers rather than ancillary segments.

Catalysts (Next 12 Months)

  • Acceleration in AWS growth from AI-related workloads and new enterprise contracts.
  • Continued advertising monetization across Prime Video and Amazon DSP network.
  • Margin expansion from fulfillment automation and cost optimization initiatives.

Risks

  • Macroeconomic slowdown impacting discretionary consumer spending and e-commerce volumes.
  • Regulatory scrutiny across antitrust, data privacy, and labor practices could constrain flexibility.
  • Competitive pressure from Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud in AI and cloud services.
