Update Note

Acer Inc. (2353.TW)

Acer is positioned to benefit from a rebound in global PC demand and diversification into AI-enabled computing and smart devices.

Investment Thesis

  • Acer’s strategic focus on AI PCs and gaming systems positions it for margin expansion as global PC replacement cycles accelerate post-2024.
  • Operational efficiency and lean inventory management have improved cash flow resilience amid volatile component pricing.
  • Valuation remains attractive relative to peers, with Acer trading at a discount to global PC OEMs despite a stronger balance sheet and improving ROE.

Key Highlights

  • Q4 2024 results showed revenue growth of 12% YoY, driven by strong gaming and commercial PC segments.
  • Gross margin expanded by 80bps to 13.5%, reflecting improved product mix and cost discipline.
  • Management reaffirmed its commitment to diversify into AI computing, e-mobility, and digital services.

Valuation & Methodology

We value Acer using a blended approach combining EV/EBITDA and P/E multiples relative to global peers such as HP Inc. and Lenovo Group. Applying a 7.5x FY2025E EV/EBITDA multiple and a 12x P/E multiple yields an implied target price of TWD 42 per share.

This valuation reflects Acer’s improving profitability, strong free cash flow generation, and exposure to AI-enabled computing growth. Despite cyclical headwinds in consumer electronics, Acer’s diversified product portfolio and disciplined cost structure support sustainable earnings growth through 2026.

Catalysts (Next 12 Months)

  • Launch of AI PC lineup and integration of generative AI features across product categories.
  • Recovery in global PC shipments as enterprise refresh cycles resume.
  • Potential spin-off or strategic partnership in Acer’s e-mobility and cloud service divisions.

Risks

  • Prolonged weakness in consumer demand or delayed enterprise upgrade cycles could pressure revenue growth.
  • Component cost inflation or supply chain disruptions may compress margins.
  • Intensifying competition from Chinese OEMs could limit pricing power in emerging markets.
