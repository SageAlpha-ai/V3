Update Note

Daikin Industries Ltd. (6367 JP)

Daikin remains the global leader in HVAC systems, positioned to benefit from structural demand for energy-efficient cooling and heat pump solutions.

Investment Thesis

  • Daikin is the world’s largest manufacturer of air conditioning systems, supported by strong brand equity, proprietary inverter technology, and global distribution scale.
  • Structural tailwinds from decarbonization and electrification are driving demand for heat pumps, where Daikin holds a leading market position in Europe and Japan.
  • Margin expansion is expected through product mix improvement, localized production in emerging markets, and digital service monetization.

Key Highlights

  • FY2024 revenue growth of ~6% YoY driven by resilient residential and commercial HVAC demand despite macro softness in China.
  • Operating margin recovery supported by easing raw material costs and improved pricing discipline.
  • Continued investment in R&D and sustainability initiatives, including refrigerant transition and smart HVAC systems.

Valuation & Methodology

We value Daikin using a blended approach of forward P/E and EV/EBITDA multiples relative to global peers such as Carrier, Trane Technologies, and Mitsubishi Electric. Our 12-month target price of ¥26,500 implies a 22x FY2025E P/E multiple, slightly above the peer median, justified by Daikin’s superior ROIC, technology leadership, and exposure to long-term energy transition themes.

DCF valuation supports similar upside, assuming mid-single-digit revenue CAGR, stable EBITDA margins near 15%, and a WACC of 7.5%. The stock trades at ~19x FY2025E earnings, offering a favorable risk/reward profile given secular growth visibility.

Catalysts (Next 12 Months)

  • Acceleration of heat pump adoption in Europe under EU decarbonization policy support.
  • Launch of next-generation refrigerant and smart HVAC systems improving energy efficiency.
  • Potential margin uplift from cost optimization and supply chain localization in Southeast Asia.

Risks

  • Exposure to cyclical demand in construction and real estate sectors, particularly in China and North America.
  • Foreign exchange volatility affecting profitability given Daikin’s global operations.
  • Competitive pressure from regional players and potential regulatory shifts in refrigerant standards.
